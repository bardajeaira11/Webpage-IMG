SPORTS

organized, competitive physical activities or games, often governed by rules, aimed at maintaining or improving physical ability and skills while providing entertainment.

BASKETBALL

a fast-paced team sport where two teams of five players compete on a rectangular court to score points by shooting a ball through a 10-foot-high hoop.

ARCHERY

the art, sport, or skill of shooting arrows with a bow, traditionally used for hunting or combat, and now primarily as a competitive sport or recreational hobby.

VOLLEYBALL

a team sport where two teams of six players use their hands to hit a ball over a high net, aiming to land it on the opponent's court.

FOOTBALL

a team sport played with a ball—usually round for soccer or oval for American/Rugby—where two teams compete to advance the ball into the opponent's goal or scoring area.

Arnis | MARTIAL ARTS

the official national martial art and sport of the Philippines, focusing on weapon-based fighting with rattan sticks, blades, and knives. It emphasizes practical self-defense, incorporating empty-hand techniques, joint locks, and grappling.

BASEBALL

a bat-and-ball sport played between two teams of nine players who take turns batting and fielding, aiming to score runs by advancing counterclockwise around four bases arranged in a diamond.

BILLIARDS

a cue sport played on a large, felt-covered rectangular table with cushioned rails, where players use a stick (cue) to strike hard balls.

BADMINTON

a fast-paced racket sport, played either individually (singles) or in pairs (doubles), where players use rackets to hit a shuttlecock across a high net, aiming to land it within the opponent's court.

BOXING

a combat sport where two opponents wearing padded gloves exchange punches, aiming to knock out or outscore each other within a roped ring, overseen by a referee.

CRICKET

a bat-and-ball sport played between two teams of eleven players, or a leaping insect known for its chirping sound.

CYCLING

the act, sport, or recreation of riding a bicycle or other pedal-driven, human-powered vehicles, such as unicycles or tricycles.

GOLF

an outdoor sport where players use various clubs to hit a small, hard ball into a series of 9 or 18 holes on a course, using as few strokes as possible.

GYMNASTIC

physical exercises that build strength, agility, and coordination, or a competitive sport involving, apparatus such as bars, beams, and rings.

MARATHON

a long-distance foot race with a distance of 42.195 kilometres, usually run as a road race, but the distance can be covered on trail routes.

POLE VAULT

a track and field event where athletes sprint down a runway and use a long, flexible pole to vault themselves over a high horizontal bar.

RUGBY

A sport played by two teams of 15 or 7 players who try to score points by carrying, passing, and kicking an oval ball over the opponent's goal line.

SKATING

the sport or activity of gliding across a surface—such as ice, concrete, or wood—using specialized boots equipped with blades or wheels.

SWIMMING

the propulsion of the body through water using arms and legs, practiced for recreation, sport, exercise, or survival. It is a low-impact, full-body activity that improves cardiovascular health and strength.

TAEKWONDO

a Korean martial art translating to "the way of the foot and fist" ("tae" = foot/kick, "kwon" = fist/punch, "do" = way/discipline). It focuses on high, fast, spinning kicks, self-defense, and mental discipline.

TENNIS

a sport played individually (singles) or in pairs (doubles) where players use strung rackets to hit a hollow, felt-covered rubber ball over a net onto a rectangular court.

WRESTLING

a combat sport or physical contest where two unarmed individuals grapple, attempting to subdue, throw, or pin each other to the ground without striking blows.
